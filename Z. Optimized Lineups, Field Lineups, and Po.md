Optimized Lineups, Field Lineups, and Portfolio Lineups

We're creating files containing three types of lineups:
    1. Optimized Lineups: these lineups are generated by a lineup optimizer to maximize mean projected fantasy points given a series of constraints.
    2. Field Lineups: these lineups are generated by a random lineup generator to replicate the competition. Uses ownership projections and salary.
    3. Portfolio Lineups: these lineups are chosen from the group of optimized lineups based on their performance relative to the field lineups. 

Stacking:
    Optimized Lineups: should create whatever stacks perform best against the field
    Field Lineups: should create stacks to resemble the field (and we already have this!)
    Portfolio: should be a subset of the optimized lineups

Challenges:
    Optimized Lineups: benefit from multithreaded runs, but 
    Field Lineups: just haven't implemented other stack types yet

Parallelism:
    The right approach for using parallelism to maximize speed may differ depending on if we're running day-of or backtesting
        Day of: want to create day's stacks concurrently.
        Backtesting: want to create day's stacks subsequently.
    Solutions:
        Focus on day of: speed is most pressing when generating lineups before games
        Focus on backtesting: speed increases have the biggest time impact here
        Eat cake: parallelize across stacks day of and contests for backtesting
